Walk into almost any salon this season and you'll notice a shift away from long, dramatic sets toward finishes that read as polished but understated. Technicians say requests have moved quickly, and menus are adjusting to keep up.

**Chrome and glazed finishes.** Mirror-like chrome powders remain popular, but the newer version is softer — a "glazed donut" sheen applied over a neutral base rather than a full metallic color.

**Micro french tips.** The classic french manicure is back, shrunk down to a thin line at the tip instead of a full white edge. It pairs well with short, natural-length nails.

**Textured and matte top coats.** Sand, suede, and other matte textures are showing up as a way to make simple color shades feel more current without adding nail art.

**Single-accent nails.** Rather than a full art design, many clients are asking for one accent nail with a subtle design, keeping the rest of the set solid and low-maintenance.

**Milky and "jelly" shades.** Sheer, semi-transparent pinks and whites continue to be requested for both natural and extended nails, especially for daytime and workwear looks.

Most of these trends share a common thread: lower maintenance, shorter appointment times, and finishes that grow out gracefully between fills.
