Like a lot of fast-growing suburbs near larger metro areas, Vancouver, Washington has seen its nail and beauty scene expand quickly over the past several years, with salons ranging from quick walk-in shops to full-service spa suites. More options is generally good for clients, but it also means pricing, service menus, and quality vary more from one salon to the next than in a smaller, more established market.

**Confirm the price of your specific service before you sit down.** Menu prices and promotional or gift-card pricing don't always match what's rung up at checkout, especially once add-ons like extra callus removal, gel versus regular polish, or nail length upcharges come into play. Asking for a full quote up front, out loud, is the simplest way to avoid a surprise total.

**Ask what's included before agreeing to an "upgrade."** It's common for a technician to suggest a VIP pedicure, deluxe treatment, or additional service mid-appointment. That's not inherently a red flag, but it's worth getting a clear price before saying yes, rather than finding out at checkout.

**Check how recent the reviews you're reading actually are.** A salon's overall star rating can look strong while individual complaints — about wait times, pricing, or inconsistent technicians — cluster in a specific recent period. Reading a mix of recent and older reviews gives a more complete picture than the star average alone.

**Licensing is checkable, not just a matter of trust.** Washington's Department of Licensing maintains a public lookup for cosmetology and manicurist licenses, so if you want to confirm a salon's technicians are currently licensed, that information is a search away rather than something you have to take on faith.

None of this is specific to Vancouver — it's the same due diligence that makes sense before booking at any salon, in any growing market. The larger the number of options in an area, the more it pays off to spend two minutes confirming price and scope before the service starts.
