Both gel polish and dip (SNS-style) powder are marketed as longer-lasting alternatives to regular polish, but they hold up differently depending on your daily routine.

**Gel polish** is applied in thin layers and cured under a UV or LED lamp between coats. It tends to have a glossier, thinner finish that looks closer to a traditional manicure. Average wear time is roughly two to three weeks before noticeable lifting or chipping at the tips.

**Dip powder** builds up in layers using a colored powder and a bonding liquid, without a lamp. It creates a noticeably harder, more durable shell that many clients find holds up better against daily wear like typing, dishwashing, or gym use — often three to four weeks between fills.

**Removal matters more than people expect.** Both systems require soaking in acetone to remove properly. Picking or peeling either one off — rather than soaking — is the most common cause of nail damage people associate with long-wear manicures.

**Application time differs too.** Dip powder application is often slightly faster since there's no curing time between coats, while gel allows more precise color control and thinner, more natural-looking layers.

Neither option is universally "better" — the right choice usually comes down to how hard on your hands your week tends to be, and whether you prioritize a glossier look or maximum durability.
