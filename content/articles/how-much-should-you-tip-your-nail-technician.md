Tipping at nail salons tends to follow the same general range as other personal-care services in the U.S. — commonly 15 to 20 percent of the pre-tax total — but there are a few nuances specific to nail care worth knowing.

**Base the tip on the service total, not the membership or package price.** If you're using a prepaid package or monthly membership, tip on the standard price of the service, not a discounted rate.

**Complex nail art usually earns more.** A detailed hand-painted design or intricate 3D art takes significantly longer than a basic polish change, and many clients tip a flat amount on top of the percentage to reflect the extra time and skill.

**Cash is still preferred by many technicians.** Card tips are sometimes pooled or subject to processing fees depending on the salon, so cash goes directly to the person who did the work in most cases.

**Multiple technicians, multiple tips.** If one person does your manicure and another does your pedicure, it's common (and appreciated) to tip each separately rather than combining it into one lump sum.

When in doubt, 20 percent is a safe default for good service, with adjustments up for exceptional detail work or down slightly if a service was rushed or below expectations.
