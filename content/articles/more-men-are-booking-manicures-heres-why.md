Salons in many markets report a steady rise in male clients booking manicures and pedicures, often framed less around polish and more around basic hand and foot care.

**"Grooming" menus are replacing traditional labels.** Rather than listing services strictly as "manicure" or "pedicure," many salons now offer a men's grooming menu covering cuticle care, callus treatment, and a clear or matte finish, which seems to lower the barrier for first-time clients.

**Word of mouth plays a big role.** Many men say they first tried a service after a friend, partner, or coworker recommended it, rather than seeing it advertised directly.

**It's often paired with other self-care services.** Men's grooming appointments frequently get booked alongside a haircut, beard trim, or massage rather than as a standalone visit, especially at salons or spas that offer combined service packages.

**Comfort matters as much as the service itself.** Salons that have seen the most growth in male clientele often point to small changes — private stations, gender-neutral menu language, or simply having male clients visible in the space — as more influential than price or marketing.

The overall service itself hasn't changed much; what's changed is how it's presented and who feels comfortable walking in the door.
