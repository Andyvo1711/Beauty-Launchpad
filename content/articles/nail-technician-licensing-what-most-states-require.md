Every U.S. state regulates nail technicians in some form, though the specific hour requirements and exam structure vary from state to state.

**Training hours typically range from roughly 100 to 600.** Some states set a relatively low hour minimum focused specifically on nail services, while others require a broader cosmetology license that also covers hair and skin, which comes with a much longer training requirement.

**Written and practical exams are standard.** Most states require passing both a written test covering sanitation, safety, and basic anatomy, and a hands-on practical exam demonstrating technique on a live model or mannequin.

**Continuing education isn't universal, but it's growing.** A number of states now require periodic renewal courses covering updated sanitation standards or product safety, on top of the initial licensing exam.

**Salons themselves are usually licensed separately from individual technicians.** A salon's business license and a technician's individual license are two different things — a legitimate business license doesn't confirm that every technician working there is currently licensed.

**Checking a license is usually simple.** Most state cosmetology boards maintain a public license lookup tool online, where a client can verify an individual technician's license status by name.

If a salon or technician can't produce a current license on request, most state boards consider that a legitimate reason to raise a concern or file a complaint.
