A quiet but significant shift in the nail industry over the past several years has been the spread of salon-specific point-of-sale software, which is changing how revenue and tips get tracked day to day.

**Card and app-based tipping now leaves an automatic record.** As more clients tip through a card reader or a salon's booking app instead of cash, that portion of tip income is captured automatically rather than depending on a technician to record it manually.

**Per-technician sales tracking simplifies commission math and reporting.** Modern systems can attribute each service and product sale to the specific technician who performed it, which makes commission payouts and individual income reporting far more precise than manual booking logs.

**Automated daily reconciliation catches errors early.** Systems that compare recorded sales against the cash drawer or deposit total at the end of each day surface discrepancies immediately, instead of leaving them to surface months later during a tax filing or audit.

**Cash still requires a manual step, and that's where gaps remain.** Even the best software can't automatically verify cash a client hands directly to a technician without going through the register — closing that specific gap still depends on salon policy and staff habits rather than the software itself.

For salons that have adopted these systems fully, tax season and the occasional audit have reportedly become far less stressful — largely because the reconciliation work happens continuously instead of being reconstructed after the fact.
